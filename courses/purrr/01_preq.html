Functions

The Principia begins with a definition. Isaac Newton is stating mass (quantity of matter) is equal to (the measure of the same) its density and volume (bulk) multiplied (combined).

This definition, or statement of fact, is also known as a function. You are intimately familiar with functions, even if you dont know it yet. By the end of this chapter, I hope to influence the way you think about solving problems in the light of functional programming.

Newtons definition can be defined in R.

calculate_mass <- function(density, volume) {
    density * volume
}

calculate_mass(2, 2)
## [1] 4
